Calculating Energy Deficit For One Pound Of Weight Loss

by Count Rugen

Posted on April 15, 2017

I recently ran into this great article:

What is the Required Energy Deficit per unit Weight Loss?

In it, the author gives an equation for finding the number of calories required for a kg of weight loss.

You’ve probably heard the old rule of thumb that a calorie deficit of 3,500 kcals equals one pound of fat loss… well, that turns out to be a pretty good estimate for obese people, but as you get leaner, you need less of a caloric deficit.

The equation

I’ve implemented the equation in python. You’ll need to install the scipy library.

from scipy.special import lambertw
from math import exp

#dBW = change in bodyweight in kg
#Fi = Fat Mass initial in kg
pL = 7.6 #density of lean tissue MJ/kg
pF = 39.5 #density of fat MJ/kg
#Note: 1 MJ/kg = 239.006 kcal/2.2 lbs = 108.639 kcal/lb


lambertMe = (1/10.4)*exp(dBW/10.4)*Fi*exp(Fi/10.4)
lambertValue = lambertw(lambertMe)

leanLossRatio = 1 + (Fi/dBW) - ((10.4/dBW) * lambertValue)

energyWeightRatio = pF + ((pL - pF) * leanLossRatio) #in MJ/kg

kCalsPerPound = round(energyWeightRatio.real * 108.639)

You need to supply the values for Fi (initial fat mass in kg) and dBW (change in bodyweight in kg). 1 kg = 2.2 lb

Uses

This equation is handy for calculating your maintenance calories. There are a lot of equations out there for this, but they give pretty rough estimates and you usually have to guess at what activity scaling factor to use.

This equation allows you to work backwards. Plot your weight loss and body fat over time along with the calories you eat each day. Then use the equation to calculate your caloric deficit for each pound of body fat lost.

Then calculate the total caloric deficit for the period, add it to the total calories consumed during that time, and divide by the number of days. That gives you your daily maintenance kcals.

No activity levels required. You let your own weight loss tell you what your maintenance calories are.

Once you know your maintenance calories, you can use the results of this equation to tell you how many calories you need to eat in order to meet your weight loss goals.

Let’s be realistic

OK, so let’s not kid ourselves about the accuracy of these calculations. We’ll probably get a better estimate of our maintenance calories than if we guessed at an activity level and used one of the existing equations, but we’re still working with a rough estimate.

Still, for those of you who like working with the numbers, this is probably about as accurate as you’re going to get.
