Liquidating My Individual Investment Accounts

by humperdinck

Posted on April 10, 2018

As a young investor with dreams of increasing my fortune through investing, I put a lot of my money in an individual (taxable) investment account. This meant I could (hopefully) grow my funds, but still have access to them; unlike with a (tax-sheltered) retirement account. I knew that a better place for these funds was probably a retirement account, but I just wasn’t ready to let go of them like that.

Well, I’ve finally decided to do the mature thing. I’m liquidating my individual investment accounts and all that money will go toward maxing out my retirement account contributions and filling up my emergency accounts.

This includes selling off all of my MLPs. These have some tax advantages and good yields, but they have been a real pain to deal with around tax time. Selling them has some tax implications as well; it’s probably going to be a pain in the ass to figure out how to report the sale next year (and a smaller inconvenience to account for the sale of the regular equities).
