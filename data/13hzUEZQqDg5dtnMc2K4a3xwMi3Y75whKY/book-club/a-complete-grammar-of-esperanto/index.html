Buttercup’s Review

This was a pretty good reference, but some of the explanations were difficult to understand. A few were nearly impossible.

This happens sometimes with old books as ways of describing things and common knowledge changes over time.

This book is useful because you can find it in a format that will easily convert to Kindle format, but I’d suggest the American Esperanto Handbook (search Archive.org) for anyone studying the language.

I think the American Esperanto Handbook does a better job of organizing the grammar and includes the lessons from Zamenhof’s Fundamento. Those lessons will use simple vocabulary to gradually teach you Esperanto in Esperanto. Unfortunately, all copies I know of are scanned page images.

Description

Public domain Esperanto grammar text easily found on Project Gutenberg, Archive.org, or Google Books.

Goes through the essential grammar of the language and provides vocabulary.
