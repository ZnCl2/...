Prince Humperdinck’s Review

I liked it.

Skip to chapter 7 to get the details and then go back to previous chapters if anything didn’t made sense or you need to be sold on the idea of investing for dividends in the first place.

The basic criteria for choosing investments are:

  • Dividend Yield >= 4.7%
  • Average Annualized Dividend Growth over the last 10 years >= 10%
  • Annual Dividends / Free Cash Flow per Share < 75% (Except for MLPs and REITs)

Ideally, this will find stable, profitable, and boring companies that will continue to increase their dividends well into the future. You would only sell if dividends become an unsustainable percent of free cash flow (> 75%).

Seems pretty simple, mechanical, and reasonable to me; all good things for an investment strategy.
