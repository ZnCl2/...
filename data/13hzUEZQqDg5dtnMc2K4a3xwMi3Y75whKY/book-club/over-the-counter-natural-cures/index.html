Buttercup’s Review

As usual, with the people’s chemist, there’s some good information here. Footnotes are there to check out his sources.

Each chapter boils down to one or two supplementation reccomendations. I wish he’d get to the point and just list them in the beginning. Instead you’ll wade through descriptions of the problem, problems with prescription meds, and occasionally the familiar trope of describing some friend/client of the author who had the problem and then the author’s brilliant advice made everything better.

I noticed a few mistakes here and there which make me question whether he got everything else right, but you can always follow up on his sources. (e.g. He claims Wellbutrin is a selective seritonin re-uptake inhibitor, while it’s actually a norepinephrine-dopamine reuptake inhibitor; that’s a big mistake in my book.)

Key Recommendations

  • Antioxidants/Skincare: 4mg/kg ALA (bonus add acetyl-L-carnitine)
  • Cholesterol: L-arginine, grape-seed extract, at least 500 mg folate/folic acid + other B vitamins (brewer’s yeast is a good source)
  • Blood Pressure/Clots: Hawthorn (1-3% bioflavanoids) 15-20 mg/kg 2-3x/day; 500-1,000 mg Magnesium
  • Insomnia: Valerian 500mg/40 lb body weight 1 hour before bed.
  • Illnesses: Eat a raw garlic clove 3 times a day; Andrographis (standardized 4-6%) 500-1,000 mg with food.
  • Blood Sugar/Obesity: Cinnamon before every meal; water + lemon juice between meals to curb hunger.
