Stop Disrespecting Rosemary!

by Vizzini

Posted on December 29, 2018

Have you ever used rosemary in your cooking? Do you know how strong this herb is?

It’s very strong. A little of this stuff goes a long way. A very long way. It’s very easy to over-power a dish with too much rosemary; you only need a few leaves.

Then why, oh why, do I keep seeing people throw whole sprigs of this shit on their cooking!?

Mrs. Vizzini does this, and it drives me nuts!

Here’s what people do: They want some rosemary in their dish, so instead of crumpling or chopping up a few leaves and throwing on a pinch or two (which would be very effective), they cut a few 6-inch-long segments off of the plant and sort of throw them in somewhere on the side.

Of course, they have to throw in the rosemary un-crushed and un-chopped, because otherwise, the flavor would be too strong. The only way you can use this much rosemary without ruining your food is to treat that stuff gently so that only a tiny fraction of the flavor will leak out.

The vast majority of the flavor goes unused and the sprigs get thrown out in the trash, but eww… doesn’t it look nice?

What the fuck, people? Not only are you wasting this herb, but you’re ripping off large parts of the plant just for decoration! This isn’t a fast grower!

It just seems so disrespectful to the plant to me. Take what you need from the plant and use it. That plant’s a living thing; don’t hack of 6-inch-long pieces of it just so you can have a fucking decoration!

Roses (and other perennial flowers) are a different story. They grow back very quickly after being pruned, and you sort of have to prune them from time to time or they start looking crappy.
