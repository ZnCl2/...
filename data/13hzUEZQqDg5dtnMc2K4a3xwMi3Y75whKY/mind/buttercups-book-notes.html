Buttercup's Book Club

by Buttercup

Posted on January 18, 2018

I mentioned in an earlier post that I was thinking about starting a project to share notes from books, and turn them into flashcards for Anki.

Well, it’s been almost a year and I never did get around to it, but it’s still on my list of things to do.

Inspired by Derek Sivers, I decided to publish the highlights from my Kindle.

These are all automatically pulled from my Kindle’s highlights file, and they’re just snippets of text that I’ve highlighted/underlined while reading these books. Because of that, it’s sometimes difficult to tell what a passage is referring to, but you can usually figure it out from the context. It’s almost as if authors don’t write books so that you can snip out neat self-contained quotes.

Also, with many of these books, I never finished reading them. I may have given up because I got bored, or too busy, or I may still be working through them.

And some of these books are things I pieced together with the Grab My Book addon for firefox, or converted from a Project Gutenberg text, so you won’t be able to find all of them on Amazon.

Introducing…

Buttercup’s Book Club

Hopefully, this will be a first step towards the project.
