Concept of money

Top ten terms

  1. Money : Any item and record acceptable for goods and services or for repayments of debts.
  2. Currency : When money is in actual use or circulation as a medium of exchange.
  3. Coins : A piece of actual metal which is standardize, and use as medium of exchange.
  4. Cash : Either money in physical currency form, or current assets accessible immediately.
  5. Wealth : It is the abundance of valuable material possessions or resources.
  6. Commodities : It is any substantially fungible, marketable item that satisfy wants or needs.
  7. Property : Something that belongs to someone either privately or publicly or collectively.
  8. Good and bad money : When two forms of commodity money are in circulation and as per law both have the same face value, the more valuable commodity is called good money.
  9. Virtual money : Any unregulated digital money, issued and controlled by its developers and which is used within a specific virtual community.
  10. Fortune : Big property and abundance of wealth.E.g. A successful IPO can make the promoters can make their fortune.

Gift economy

In most basic survival societies, few goods are produced. Needs are limited. Wants are non-existence. Hence valuables are neither traded or sold, but are given. No rewards are expected. E.g. Giving arms to Buddhist monks.(Thervad Buddhism , Thailand); dana-puniya in Hinduism(To rid one-self from sins)

Batter economy

Goods and services are directly exchanged for other goods and service without any medium of exchange. The exchange is immediate and is not delayed.

Negatives of batter system

  1. An inefficient system due to “double coincidence of wants”.
  2. No common measure of value.
  3. Differed payments problem.
  4. Storing wealth very difficult.

Positives of batter system

  1. Very useful in hyper-inflation (especially for poor).
  2. Very good for unknown parties.

Money and its four functions

A per Williams Stanley Jevons, the four functions are:

Money's is a matter of functions four
A medium,a measure,standard,a store.
  1. A medium of exchange- can easily compare the values of dissimilar.
  2. A measure of values- It is standard common denomination of trade.
  3. A standard of deferred payment- Use to settle debts(but it can be badly effected by inflation,devaluation,etc.)
  4. A store of value- It can reliably saved,stored and retrieved.

Three is now considered part of 1,2 and 4.

Money supply

In economics, money referees to any financial instrument that can fulfill the four functions of money. If you consider all these financial instruments, it becomes the nations money supply.Thus money supply is the total amount of monetary assets at given time.If you add up the money supply of all these, it becomes the nations monetary aggregate. 

Money supply =  Currency,bank notes or coins + Bank money

Bank money is the biggest component of money supply.

Due to the presence of the CRR/SLR etc.,Indian banking is called fractional reserve banking.

  4. E.g. shells,cocoa beans,salt,copper,gold

Gresham's law

Bad money always drives out good money.For E.g. If two types of commodity money are in circulation,and as per law both of them have same face value, the more valuable commodity will disappear.
