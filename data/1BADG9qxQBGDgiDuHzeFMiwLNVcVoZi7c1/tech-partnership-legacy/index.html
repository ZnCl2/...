Tech Partnership Legacy

Thank you for your interest in the Tech Partnership. Although the organisation has now closed, many of its activities are being taken forward by others, as set out below. Please make contact with them if you are interested in digital skills.

 

Higher Education (Degrees & Degree Apprenticeships) >>> Tech Partnership Degrees

The collective work of employers and universities continues, with all Higher Education activities – including degrees, degree apprenticeships and Tech Industry Gold accreditation – being taken forward by the new not-for-profit Tech Partnership Degrees.

If you are interested in industry backed degree apprenticeships or degrees, including IT Management for Business (ITMB) and Software Engineering for Business (SEfB), please register to get updates at www.tpdegrees.com or contact Bob Clift on bob.clift@tpdegrees.com, tel 07766 523530.

Whilst work on degree apprenticeships continues through Tech Partnership Degrees, the organisations work on apprenticeships levels 2, 3 and 4 has been transferred to three companies:

NSAR, the National Skills Academy for Rail, is supporting employers on the External Quality Assurance of apprenticeships at levels 2, 3 and 4, to ensure parity of end point assessment across different awarding organisations. Contact is via enquiries@nsar.co.uk, tel 0203 021 0575.

Instructus Skills is responsible for certification for digital apprenticeship frameworks. Contact is via skills@instructus.org, tel 01536 738631.

Essential Digital Skills >>> The Department for Education

The Essential Digital Skills Framework defines the digital skills adults need to safely benefit from, participate in and contribute to the digital world. This has been transferred to the Crown, with contact via essential.digitalskills@education.gov.uk.

 

Thank you for your interest. We hope you will get involved with the new organisations to continue to help the UK develop the digital skills it needs for the future.
