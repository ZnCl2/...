The Scripture of the Old and the New Testament is the word of God.

Why was the Bible written?

To know the will of God and to do it, to know the grace of God, and to have delighted in it.

The holy scripture is called the Bible. It is a Greek word which means books - it is the book of books.

The Bible is divided between two sections:

  • The Old Testament, written before the birth of Jesus Christ.
  • The New Testament, written after the birth of Jesus Christ.

The Bible consists of 79 books:

  • 66 canonical books.
  • 13 deuterocanonical / apocrypha books. In most Protestant Bibles, these books are not included.

The Old Testament contains 39 books

17 historic books:

  • 5 books of Moses,
  • Joshua, Judges, Ruth,
  • 2 books of Samuel, 2 books of kings,
  • 2 books of chronicles, Book of Ezra,
  • Book of Nehemiah, Book of Esther.
5 teaching books:
  • Book of Job, Book of Psalms,
  • Book of Proverbs, Book of Ecclesiastes,
  • Song of Salomon.
4 books of major prophets:
  • Book of Isaiah,
  • Book of Jeremiah and Lamentations,
  • Book of Ezekiel,
  • Book of Daniel.
12 books of minor prophets:
  • Book of Hosea, Book of Joel,
  • Book of Amos, Book of Obadiah,
  • Book of Jonah, Book of Micah,
  • Book of Nahum, Book of Habakkuk,
  • Book of Zephaniah, Book of Haggai,
  • Book of Zechariah, Book of Malachi.

The New Testament contains 27 books

5 historic books:

  • Gospel of Matthew, Gospel of Mark,
  • Gospel of Luke, Gospel of John,
  • Book of Acts.
21 teaching books:
  • Epistle of Paul to Romans,
  • 2 Epistles of Paul to Corinthians,
  • Epistle of Paul to Galatians,
  • Epistle of Paul to Ephesians,
  • Epistle of Paul to Philippians,
  • Epistle of Paul to Colossians,
  • 2 Epistles of Paul to Thessalonians,
  • 2 Epistles of Paul to Timothy,
  • Epistle of Paul to Titus,
  • Epistle of Paul to Philemon,
  • Epistle to Hebrews,
  • Epistle of James,
  • 2 Epistles of Peter,
  • 3 Epistles of John,
  • Epistle of Jude.
1 prophetic book:
  • Revelation of John.

Apocrypha books

These books were canonized (with some minor differences) by Catholic and Orthodox Church. Protestant churches consider them as apocrypha. There are 13 apocrypha books:

  • Book of Tobit, Prayer of Manasseh,
  • Book of Judith, Book of Baruch,
  • Additions to Daniel, Additions to Esther,
  • 2 books of Ezra,
  • 3 books of Maccabees,
  • Wisdom of Solomon, Book of Sirach.

What did the Lord God write in the Old Testament?

In particular, he wrote down his holy will according to which we have to live and what he asks of us. Through Moses, He issued the 10 commandments of God. Through the prophets, He brought up the people of Israel in preparation for the birth of Christ, and gave the promise that he will send the Messiah to save the people.

What did the Lord God write in the New Testament?

In the New Testament, the grace of God in Christ is revealed to us, a joyful message is announced to us, that the heavenly Father sent the Messiah, Jesus Christ, who prepared salvation to all people and calls them back to Him.
