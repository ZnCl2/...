Frank Einstein and the Antimatter Motor

October 12, 2018

Frank Einstein and the Antimatter Motor by Jon Scieszka
My rating: 5 of 5 stars

My five year old read it in two weeks, it was a fun light read.
While it is a soft sci-fi with many fantastical things happening,
it does include some real science as an entertaining introduction.
For me it checks off all the boxes, it has robots, is educational,
is good for my child’s reading development, and is entertaining.
So I give it 5 stars, well done Jon Scieszka!

View all my reviews

Part one vision, is mostly about startups in early-adopter phase, with lots of experimentation. It helped raise my confidence that I don’t need a perfect product, or even a very good one, just one that works, and then can try it out.
Part two vision is about applying the scientific method an doing A/B testing on everything, also covers a bunch of different ways to pivot, which I think is probably one of the most innovative and best parts of this book.
Part three accelerate has some tips on how to get things accomplished, like using small batches instead of large ones.

The main reason I took off a star is because the title isn’t super accurate, while it does say “lean startup”, it includes a lot of information for mega-corporations as well, and the main message throughout the book is “use the scientific method”.

I think perhaps a more accurate title would be “innovating in business using the scientific method”.
All that said, I have figuring out wasabi A/B testing framework firmly on my todo list, and will schedule some time to figure it out soon.
