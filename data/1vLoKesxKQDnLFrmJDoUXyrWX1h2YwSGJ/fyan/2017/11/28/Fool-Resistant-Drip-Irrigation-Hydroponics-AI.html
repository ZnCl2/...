Fool-Resistant Low Maintenance AI Drip Irrigation Hydroponics System

November 28, 2017

Canaba proud and free

Canada is introducing new legislation by July 2018, that will allow adult Canadians to grow up to 4 Marijuana plants in their household, or on their property. For many this may be their first attempt at growing a plant, and they may want a fool-resistance low-mainteance and high efficiency drip system, which can be used both indoors and outdoors.

While Canadians will also be allowed to buy Marijuana from federal or provincial retailers. Marijuana is sufficiently expensive on a per-gram basis that it is cost-effective to grow your own. For example under the legislation each person is allowed to carry around up to 30 grams of Marijuana. At $10 per gram, that is $300 worth of marijuana.

Cost-effectiveness

An average joint has roughly a third of a gram of Marijuana. About 8\% of people consume marijuana a few times a week. If that amounts to 3 joints, or a gram a week, then it could be expected they consume 52 grams a year, with perhaps more on special occasions, so can round it to 60, which would mean any system that costs less than $600 per year, would be more economical than buying. There are roughly 29 million adults in Canada. So roughly 2.3 million regular marijuana consumers. If 65\% of those can grow in their household, that leaves 1.5 million people for whom it would be cost-effective to grow their own.

Basements

Of the people that have households, many have basements due to foundation requirements, though often they are short, since usually only need to dig the foundation 1.2m deep (4ft). Usually with some added height for windows it may give an unfinished basement a height of 1.7m, (a height of 2m is required to finish a basement).
I mention this because one of the most trendy hydroponic methods, which is the Deep Water Culture or Bubbleponics (which is usually 0.5m high) in combination with high efficiency LED lights (which are 0.1m high and have to be kept 0.6m away from the plants), leaves only 0.5-0.8m for the plants, which can grow up to 1m high.

There is a solution however, where plants are only 20cm off the ground by using a short-tote, for drip-irrigation and use an special LED light that allows it to be closer (45cm) during flowering. Giving a total of 1.8m, so it should fit in most Canadian basements. Can make it 1.7 meter by either training the plant to be under 0.9m, or by having a flatter drip collection tray.

Automated Drip Irrigation

While drip irrigation is highly efficient, it has it’s own unique issues. The biggest time-suck being figuring out how to set and adjust the timers for optimal growth.

By using a RISC-V Arduino have enough horsepower and memory to run a simple AI watering system that can be trained to give optimal results.

The primary plant-side sensor would be for soil-moisture, it should also have a ph-sensor for the resevoir and a water-level-meter. That way if the PH goes out of range, or water-level gets too low, it could send an email to the primary user to notify them of the problem.

For the AI portion, it would need some training, while it will certainly come pre-trained, people can give it additional training to suit their needs and conditions. For instance either through a web-interface or LCD with buttons, people could notify the AI if they are putting in a new plant, whether it is currently vegetative or flowering, and even what kind of plant it is. Additionally people could mention how the plant is doing when they stop by to check. The default LCD could be asking “how are the plants”, if the person says good, it can reinforce what it is doing, if they say bad, can ask them if it is a water or PH problem, if one of those, can ask if it needs to be higher or lower, then the neuro-net can be adjusted accordingly.

Domain

Of course this system isn’t only applicable to Marijuana, it could be applicable to a wide variety of plants grown by drip irrigation. Out in the field it gets a bit more difficult as sensors would have to be spread out pretty far.

However to narrow the range at least initially, will be to make a system that is for 4 plants, specializing in Marijuana.

Testing

I hope to build this system within the next year, and have it ready for the time that Marijuana is legal to grow, in order to test it out. If all goes well, could package it and sell such systems to fellow Canadians, or maybe even internationally.
