Deliberative Democracy

Sure. well the basic idea is that focusing on the good, in the present, can help you give it more energy and bring it into the future.

In terms of what is good in the present government, is democracy, at least here in Canada, don’t know where you are at. I know it’s much more ambigious in America.

Here for every bill that is passed, and anything that happens there is consultation with the people. So anyone that shows up to the council meetings, or the legislative review and drafting meetings can make their input. One of the things I’d like to do with a robotic party, is to have a robot representative attend these meetings, that way the robot-party consittuents can all make their input to these council meetings, without having to actually leave their homes and drive to city hall, or parliament hill, or queen’s park (provincial ontario legislature), in order to hear what is going on and provide their own input.

with a combination of the robot represenattive and liquid democracy it can dramatically increase the voice of the people in all bills that are passed. Currently big companies can afford to put their meat-puppets, er I mean lobbyists into these meetings to get the things they want passed. But most common people don’t have the time, energy or proximity to do so. With a robot party representative they will be able to.
