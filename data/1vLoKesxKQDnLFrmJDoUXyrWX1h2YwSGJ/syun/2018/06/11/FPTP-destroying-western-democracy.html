First Past The Post is destroying western democracy

June 11, 2018

First-past-the-post (FPTP) is a system where a minority of votes get 100% of the decision making power. It has led to countries such as America and Canada to become increasingly unstable and volatile, as one major party replaces another major party, then rips up the former parties legislation. Quite literally it is ripping these countries apart.

FPTP creates a destabilized country which can’t maintain direction for more than a few years, so it often goes in one direction, stops, reverses, goes in another direction, stops, reverses, rinse, repeat.

The top 5 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democracy_Index all use proportional representation (PR), as do over 90% of the extant full democracies. The only exceptions being Canada, UK, and Mauritius. Though I don’t know about Mauritius, the destabilizing forces of FPTP are forcing a downward trend in Canada and the UK.

While PR may not solve all problems, it helps a lot. Particularly because the norm is a coalition government, where there is a continual seeking of consensus, rather than a more authoritarian approach which is typical of FPTP systems.
