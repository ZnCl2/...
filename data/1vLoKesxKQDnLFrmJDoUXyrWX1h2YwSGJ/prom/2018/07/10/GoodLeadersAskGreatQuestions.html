Good Leaders Ask Great Questions

July 10, 2018

Good Leaders Ask Great Questions: Your Foundation for Successful Leadership by John C. Maxwell
My rating: 5 of 5 stars

The book is composed of many great questions regarding leadership and their corresponding answers.
John Maxwell clearly has had many decades of experience in leadership, and has done significant research and practical leadership training. Most of the book is entrenched in his experiences, but he also cites some other sources such as books by Jim Collins. John Maxwell certainly comes across as a Level 5 Leader (the best kind), and shares his how it can be achieved.

It’s a good book, I’d definitely recommend it to anyone that wants to be a leader in life.
Also will likely read it again when have leadership challenges,
as it has answers to many leadership questions.

here are some of my notes based on the chapter headings:

PART I: QUESTIONS I ASK
1. Why Are Questions So Important?
* “Instead of wondering what your next vacation is, maybe you should set up a life
you don’t need to escape from.”

2. What Questions Do I Ask Myself as a Leader?
* have a list of questions to ask yourself everyday
** to help maintain humility and other good leadership qualities.

3. What Questions Do I Ask My Team Members?
* what do you think?
* how can I serve you? (what can I do to help you perform at your best?)
* “It is impossible to learn anything important about anyone until we get him
or her to disagree with us; it is only in contradiction that character is
disclosed. That is why autocratic employers usually remain so ignorant about the
true nature of their subordinates.”

PART II: QUESTIONS LEADERS ASK ME
4. What Must I Do to Lead Myself Successfully?
* leaders must lead themselves before anyone else
* discuss your blind spots with your inner circle

5. How Does Leadership Work?
* leadership is getting many people to work together, many people one heart.

6. How Do I Get Started in Leadership?

  • leadership opportunities are everywhere

    7. How Do I Resolve Conflict and Lead Challenging People?
    * keep communication going or be the one to initiate it
    * ask if it is somehow your fault and if you can improve
    * the weaker person controls the relationship
    * be willing to let go of counter-productive people

    8. How Can I Succeed Working Under Poor Leadership?
    * figure out if it’s actually you that have the problem
    * find concrete evidence of fault and possible remedies
    * be prepared to move on

    9. How Can I Successfully Navigate Leadership Transitions?
    * consider succession as part of leadership, including grooming successor.

    10. How Can I Develop Leaders?
    * give them practice leading while you are still in power
    * be available to help after you have left
