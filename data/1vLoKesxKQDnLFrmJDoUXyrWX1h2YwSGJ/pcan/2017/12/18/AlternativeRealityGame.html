Robot Civilization Seed: Cyborg Fjord

December 18, 2017

A long-play ARG, which is actually the gameification of the creation of and alternative reality or Robot Civilization Seeds.

A play may be:

A freelancing teen clicks on an ad for a company that pays in megajoule pegged micropayments for small AI training and programming tasks on their smartphone. They download their app and for a while enjoy solving captchas and labeling data for pay in their free time.

As they level up the data gets more complex but also weirder. “I mean sure, I told them I was majoring in geology. But why am I labeling all these wet core drill samples, and where are they coming from?” they think to themselves. The language at the higher levels also seems to inexplicably devolve with seemingly bad grammar, even if still easy to understand.

They notice some other people online speaking in a similar way, and start to dig a little deeper. It turns out they are all using some kind of machine-readable inter-language. On the forums/group of other freelancers that work for the same company, they find out that they can get better pay, if they make their classification responses in the language, as then the linguistics-majors don’t have to do the translating. So they learn the ins and outs of the language and get an extra percentage pay on their data labeling and analysis.

They keep seeing on the newsfeeds about job automation, life without work, and all the people that can’t seem to manage even with UBI. They also hear rumours of alternative UBI communities setting up in rural areas, but people pass them off as cults and weirdos. The legal, philosophy and theology majors are always getting into heated yet surprisingly civil debate threads on the forums. They keep using that language, and congratulate each other at the end on money well made.

On their 18th birthday or when they graduate/drop-out, they get an invitation in their app to join a full time working community. They think about all the homeless drug addicts they see on the streets, people begging on the streets in business suits and decide it may be for the best. After they click yes they fill out a questionaire about what their ideal community setting is, rural, urban, forest, jungle, seaside or mountains. They pick seaside. The app gives them a route plan, showing which bus route is optimal, and gives them a bonus to pay for the tickets.

They mull it over for a while, and try to find out what they can about the location they are going to. It’s a small hamlet on the west coast, in a rural area, on the side of a mountain, in the depths of a fjord.

It had a seasonal access road, and the nearest bus stop was in a nearby city. He figured he could just grab a self-driving taxi to go the last mile once he got there.

There were two companies that had branches setup there, one in aquaponics, and one in deep sea mining. The deep sea mining branch is the address he was given. He told his parent he found a job on the west coast, they were surprised but grateful and wished him well. So he got on the bus with his few belongings.

While he was in transit his app chirped and asked if he was on the way. Having confirmed, he was informed there would be someone there to greet him and take him the rest of the way. As he got off the bus, he was momentarily stunned by the tall mountains surrounding this little town, then he saw an old hatchback, and a man with and augmented reality headset waving to him. He brought his backpack along.

He approached the man and said “Hi, I’m Bryan.” “Nice to meet you, I’m Carl. Here you’ll want these, so don’t have to repeat this later.” Carl pulled out an augmented reality set from the backseat and handed them to Bryan while taking his backpack.

Bryan put on the AR glasses and looked around. Immediately he saw labels floating above the streets with the street names, and a big bus terminal sign hangning above the bus terminal. He looked at the people streaming in and out. Many were unidentified, but others had their usernames, roles and levels floating above them. He was surprised to see how many people were playing .

He turned back to Carl, and saw he was a high level logistics expert, with a side branch in human relations. Carl had finished packing and came back around. “You know how to open a door, right? Get in.” Carl said.

Bryan slightly stunned still really from the AR, opened the door, got in and put on this seatbelt. After a little while on paved streets the road turned to gravel. The mountains rose on either side as they went down a slope to the hamlet. He could hear the soft crunch of gravel in the tires, and the car slowed down.

“Do you take neural-link nanobots?” Carl asked, holding out a handful of Grey capsules. The car was driving itself, though Carl was sitting at the wheel.

Bryan’s eyebrows rose, and his lips scrunched up. He wasn’t sure how he felt about them.

“It’s alright, you’ll need a way of interfacing with the goggles beyond just speech. Have these keyboard gloves instead.” He handed them to Bryan.

Bryan inspected them, they were clean, tough breathable material. Bryan had heard of these before. A fancier version was advertized for high-end business people and anyone that didn’t want to constantly be carrying a smart phone around.

“Once you bond them to your AR set, it’ll start you up on a tutorial. we still have a while to go.” Bryan put them on and started the tutorial. The next thing he knew they were arriving.

It looked like a small encampent at first. But the AR goggles were just going wild pointing out all the IoT devices embedded into the mountain side. Weather stations, webcams, micro hydro-power plants, wireless beacons and drone pads.

“It’s a pretty steep encampent. The flimsy stuff is up here. Down closer to the water, all the buildings are submersible, with the ocean rising as it is, wont be too long before they are underwater. Though some already are, we put them there.” Carl said, taking back the wheel as they cruised into a parking spot. “From here we walk”.

Bryan got out and saw a line of exoskeletons, and a man carrying somethings down the hill. Through his AR glasses he could see that some of the other exoskeletons were remotely operated by people in VR environments.

Bryan thought back fondly to how he had been one of those before. It was simplified of course, there was no need to show the VR any more detail than necessary, but it was nevertheless quite interesting. His exoskeleton was back in the bags, as he had to wear it to particpate. He remembered his mother complaining saying maybe he should stop pretending and move some bales of hay instead with his fancy suit. She didn’t seem to understand that he was helping pay the bills through his remote construction work. Though he did also use the exo-suit to move some bales of hay. He loved his mom, but she had remarried and sold the farm to moved in with her new lover. Now he was striking out on his own.

One of the Exobots stopped momentarily as the driver vacated it, stopping the procession, auto-pilot took over. The movements changed from a human gait, to a more formal one, tuned to efficiency and precision. Bryan wondered if they really needed humans to help, or if it was merely momentum keeping them in synch.

Carl and Bryan followed the procession down. On the way his AR unit printed Immigrant analysis complete. You’ve been authorized for on-site living quarters. Would you prefer floating, submersbile, or underground homestead arrangment? Bryan was slightly taken aback. He didn’t realize that it had taken this long for him to be processed, or that he may have been denied housing here.

“Carl, what kind of housing do you have?” Bryan asked.

Carl turned back looked him up and down and nodded. “I’m going off shift now. So I’ll just tell you as a comrade. Floating, I have a need for speed my boy. them sailing trimarans may not be the loftiest option, but it sure is nice taking a nap while sailing at fifty kilometers and hour.” he laughed to himself like it was some big joke. He leaned onto the side of the mountain, and looked down,centering himself. “For you though, I’d recommend a submersible. You’re a quiet type, seem to keep to yourself. Once you take your sub underwater the whole world quiets down, and it’s just you, the fish and the aquabots.” He paused breathing the cool, fresh mountain air. “I reckon you’d like to see where those core samples come from. If you have a submersible, you could move there if you want.” Carl turned, and continued making his way down to the docks.

Bryan stood there, thinking about those wet core samples. He was a geologist, so underground would have made sense to him. But he was so curious, where are the samples coming from? He noticed during his day dream, the Carl had gotten quite ahead. Bryan hurried down, holding the railing tightly as he went.

“Carl, Carl, wait.” he said, catching up to Carl. “Where do the core samples come from?”. he said catching his breath.

Carl laughed, he laughed so hard he held onto the railing.
“Well I guess you’ll find out.”
