Pyash Friend Joule Currency

April 15, 2017

I’ve been thinking of alternative currency things like letting municiaplities have their own currencies, based on property-tax, and possibly parking-ticket credits. they would be local currencies, that would be largely worthless to anyone that doesn’t live there, or doesn’t require services from there.

also the credits would “disappear” when they get accepted by the municipality for paying property taxes. and would be generated by the municiaplity when they need to pay local companies/people for local services.

In terms of Pyash Friends though, I’m thinking of a Joule or energy-based currency. so for instance the GPL says that it is acceptable to ask for transmission-fee with source-code. the computer would calculate the electrical and mechanical costs associated with each transaction, and add in the cost of the free transactions into the paid transactions.

i.e. free transactions would be when negotiating terms.

1.2 mJ/packet

that’s for an 800 byte packet. so 2.4MB would be about 3 joules.

assuming a standard 500% profit margin, then the price would be 15 joules.

though that’s just for the bandwidth cost. doesn’t include server resources.

a Pyash Friend, could find all the information about what hardware the computer is made of, including it’s likely replacement cost and projected life expectancy, then amortize it’s cost based on the service it provides, to come up with cost, then check SingularityNet or other the PyashMarketplace for competition in order to set its price.

buyers would just buy on credit initially, and their debt would be the money, like a promissory note.
they seller could lower QoS if it doesn’t get remunirated by the debtor, or doesn’t find a buyer for the debt.
