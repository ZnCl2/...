Drinking water from thin air in parched Jalna

by

Ranjana Diggikar



AURANGABAD: It is not the magician at work nor is it a miracle. Experts are using innovative technology to tap the vapour content in the air to meet the drinking water needs without touching the existing sources of water.

A private firm has developed a machine that condenses the water vapour in the air into clean drinking water. The firm recently donated the machines to the state government, which is planning to use them in the scarcity-hit areas.

The scarcity-hit Jalna district received three such machines, which were installed at the civil hospital, Zilla Parishad and collector's office on Saturday. Each machine has the capacity to generate around 500 litres of water a day.

State water supply and sanitation minister Babanrao Lonikar inaugurated the air water harvester (Akash Amrit) on Saturday. The company has donated 100 such machines to the state government to tackle water scarcity in the state.

Lonikar said the private company has designed the device that offers a green and clean drinking water solution. "The machine harvests clean drinking water from air. The private company has donated 100 such machines to the state government and around 10 machines will be installed in the drought-hit Jalna district," he said.

An expert from the company said the technology uses proprietary knowledge of superior condensation engineering techniques to convert atmospheric water vapour into the cleanest form of drinking water. "Unlike water filtration technologies like reverse osmosis, which require a water source, our technology does not require one," the expert said.

The device costs around Rs 8 lakh and can function in full capacity when the maximum temperature is between 20 and 45 degrees celsius. The humidity levels in the atmosphere have to be 30%, the expert said.

"The technology also offers the advantage of an instantaneous solution for drinking water as it starts producing water within minutes of installation. Depending on the prevailing humidity and temperature conditions, the system is capable of making 500 to over 1,000 litres of clean drinking water per day," the expert said.

In addition, this system does not require any additional infrastructure or skilled personnel to operate or maintain it. It is not just innovative technology but also cost effective, said the expert.

FACTS ON THE TOTAL WATER RESERVES

The total volume of water on Earth is about 1.4 billion km3. The volume of freshwater resources is around 35 million km3, or about 2.5 percent of the total volume.

Of these freshwater resources, about 24 million km3 or 70 percent is in the form of ice and permanent snow cover in mountainous regions, the Antarctic and Arctic regions.

Around 30 percent of the world's freshwater is stored underground in the form of groundwater (shallow and deep groundwater basins up to 2000 metres, soil moisture, swamp water and permafrost). This constitutes about 97 percent of all the freshwater that is potentially available for human use.

Freshwater lakes and rivers contain an estimated 105 000 km3 or around 0.3 percent of the world's freshwater.

The total usable freshwater supply for ecosystems and humans is about 200 000 km3 . The Earth's atmosphere contains approximately 13,000 km3 of water
