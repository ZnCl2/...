GB 1496582
AN ENERGY REABSORPTION ENHANCEMENT CHAMBER
 
Inventor(s):     SACHER FRANCES VON
Also published as: NL7501808 // SE7412497 //JP50114888 // BR7500933 // DE2503622

This invention relates to an energy reabsorption enhancement chamber.

It is recognized that matter and energy have been theoretically established as being one and the same. Thus, all matter is capable of emitting energy and in doing so will decompose; i.e., undergo a change in form. The rate at which matter will decompose is dependent on the internal makeup of a specific matter, as well as upon the external forces acting on it. For example, under normal conditions an organic or animated body, e.g., a human body, and an inanimate body, e.g., a stone or rock, will both decompose in time; the animated body decomposing at a much greater rate than the stone. The rate of decomposition in either case can be speeded up or slowed down by subjecting the respective matter to certain external forces, e.g., heat or cold.

Thus, the rate of decomposition of matter can be controlled to some extent by influencing the internal and external forces acting upon a body, i.e., matter.

A living body, e.g., a human body is constantly emitting cellular energy. Under normal conditions this energy has been determined to be the equivalent of a 100 watt light bulb. Under various conditions, e.g, stress, physical activity, sleep, etc., the energy emitted will increase or decrease accordingly. To maintain the energy generated emitted by a living organic body, the body must be fed food to replace the vitamins, water and minerals required to sustain its cellular activity and continued emittance of cellular energy. Under normal conditions, the food intake of an organic body is sufficient to replenish the necessary life substances to delay decomposition of the body. However, under drastic change of condition, e.g., temperature, emotions and/or physical damage to the cells, it can be virtually impossible to replenish the life sustaining necessities rapidly enough.Thus, the cells eventually die of starvation, depletion, dehydration or physical damage.

An object of this invention is to provide a physical structure which will enhance the reabsorption of the energy emitted by a body, i.e., matter be it organic or inorganic.

Another object is to provide a structure capable of effecting the retention and reabsorption of emitted cellular energy by a body.

Another object is to provide a physical structure in which the reabsorption of the energy emitted by a body can be varied by the stimulation of an external energy force on the body.

Another object is to provide a structure which will naturally influence matter so as to retard the decomposition thereof.

Another object is to provide a physical structure capable of minimizing the loss of cellular energy which a body emits.

Another object is to provide a physical structure which is relatively simple in construction and which can be readily fabricated of any suitable rigid material.

According to the present invention there is provided a chamber for receiving a body whereby energy emitted by said body can in part be reabsorbed thereby, which chamber comprises a plurality of triangular side walls connected to one another along their adjacent edges to define a chamber which is pyramidal in configuration, a reflective surface being disposed on the interior side of the side walls of the chamber for inhibiting the escape of energy emitted by the body, when disposed in the chamber, and for deflecting the emitted energy inhibited from escaping to the body to be reabsorbed thereby, and a support platform centrally disposed within the chamber interior, the platform having a supporting surface located at a level such that the plane at that level divides the internal volume of the chamber into approximately equal parts, the supporting surface supporting the body thereon, when the chamber is in use, for optimum reabsorption of the energy inhibited from escaping.

According to an embodiment of the invention there is provided a treatment chamber comprising a quadrilateral base member having equal side edges, there being a triangular side wall connected to each of the edges and extending upwardly therefrom to define an apex, the side walls being connected along their respective adjacent side edges to define a pyramidshaped closed chamber and each of the side walls having the shape of an isosceles triangle, a reflective surface lining disposed on the internal wall surfaces of the side walls, the chamber having an altitude equal to approximately the perimeter of the base member divided by 27r, a support platform centrally disposed relative to the floor of the chamber and having a top surface located at an elevation which divides the internal volume of the chamber into two equal parts, means defining an access opening to the interior of the chamber and means for closing the access opening.

With a body or matter located on the platform, the arrangement is such that the emitted internal energy of whatever molecular condition will return to the centre of the internal volume of the chamber where it can be reabsorbed by the body. The rate of reabsorption can be intensified or varied by the introduction of an external energy force, such as stimulating light and/or sound, into the defined chamber. "External" in this context refers to a source additional to the energy source which is the body itself.

Subject to the disclaimer which appears hereinafter, the invention also provides a method of enhancing the reabsorption of energy by a body emitting such energy, which method comprises positioning the body on the support platform within a chamber in accordance with the invention.

Referring to the drawings, there is shown in Figures 1 and 2 a preferred embodiment of a chamber in accordance with the invention, designated by reference numeral 10. As shown, the preferred embodiment comprises a chamber 10 formed of a plurality of triangularly shaped side walls 11 which are joined along their adjacent side edges I IA to define an apex 12. In the illustrated form, four such side walls 11 are connected to define a pyramid-shaped chamber 10. While four such side walls 11 are shown, it will be understood that more than four side walls may be joined to define the chamber 10. If desired, the side walls 11 may be connected to a base member 13 to define a chamber floor. In the illustrated embodiment, the base member 13 is a quadrilaterally-shaped member, preferably defined as a square.Depending upon application, the side walls 11 may be formed of any suitable material, e.g. wood, glass, metal, plastics, stiff paper, cardboard and the like. In the illustrated embodiment, the opposite side edges I IA of the respective sides are equal so that the side wall is defined as an isosceles triangle.

At least a portion of each internal surface of the respective side walls 11 is covered with a reflecting surface 14, e.g., a mirror to reflect the emitted energy of an object or matter M disposed within the chamber 10.

In the illustrated embodiment, the entire internal surfaces of the respective side walls 11 are covered by such reflective surfaces 14.

Disposed within the chamber there is provided a support or platform 15 adapted to support an object, body, or matter M.

Preferably the top of the platform is disposed parallel to the base at a level which is such that a plane at that height above the base would divide the internal volume V of the chamber 10 into two approximately equal parts.

It has been determined that the chamber 10 may assume any proportions depending upon the specific application. However, for optimum results, it is preferred that the altitude A of the apex or top of the chamber 10 be made approximately equal to the perimeter of the base divided by 27r or 6.2832, or any equivalent fractional application of the base perimeter divided by 27r ratio defined.

If desired the chamber 10 may be provided with an acces opening 16 to facilitate the placing and/or removal of any object or body therefrom. An appropriate closure or door 17 is provided to close the opening 16 when the chamber 10 is to be used. The configuration of the internal walls 11 and the covering reflecting surfaces 14 has, for reasons unknown, a direct influence on the free flowing movement and defraction, magnification or reflection of external structural molecular, cellular, sub-atomic, and/or atomic waves emitted by a body or matter M, and which results in the emitti#ng body M to reabsorb its own energy and thereby retard any natural decomposition thereof.

While the chamber 10 defined enhances the reabsorption of the emitted bodily energy into the emitting body M, the rate of reabsorption can be intensified or varied by the introduction of an external energy force, e.g., that of a stimulating light and/or sound into the chamber 10. This can be readily achieved by the positioning of a light source, e.g., an electric lighting device 20 within the chamber 10. In the illustrated embodiment the light source 20 may be located in the vicinity of the apex or the top of the chamber 10. Also, various indirect lighting effects may be utilized whereby the intensity, colour and/or frequency thereof can be controlled to produce various psychodelic effects to stimulate or diminish the emitted energy of the body accordingly.

To a similar extent, sound maybe employed to vary the rate a body emits its energy. As shown, the sound producing means in the form of sound emitting speakers 21 are centrally positioned. It will be understood that the speakers 21 may be connected in circuit with a suitable sound transmitting source. In the illustrated embodiment, the speakers 21 are disposed in the platform so that the sound can emanate in all directions. By controlling the intensity, colour, and/or frequency of the light and/or sound to produce the various visual and/or audio effects that can be created thereby, the amount of energy emitted by a body can be varied. In turn, the amount of energy which the body can reabsorb can be varied accordingly.
