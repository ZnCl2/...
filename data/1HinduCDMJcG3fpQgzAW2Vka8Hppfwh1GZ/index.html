Core concept of Hinduism

  1. Hinduism has more than one doctrine that define its core principle.
  2. There are some shared thoughts across all doctrines.
  3. The tremendous diversity, visible differences, multiple practices and symbols make it confusing for outsiders.(The eternity of the Bramh and Prakriti.)
  4. Despite all this there is beautiful structure to the entire Hindu thought.

Atman

Brahman is the undivided cosmic consciousness, from which all Atman evolve.Atman is the non material self from, which never changes. 

  ॐ पूर्णमदः पूर्णमिदम् पूर्णात् पूर्णमुदच्यते ।
   पूर्णस्य पूर्णमादाय पूर्णमेवावशिष्यते ॥

The Brahman is infinite and this universe is infinite. The infinite proceed from the infinite. It still remains infinite Brahman.


नैनं छिन्दन्ति शस्त्राणि नैनं दहति पावकः ।
न चैनं क्लेदयन्त्यापो न शोषयति मारुतः ।।

The soul can never be cut to pieces by any weapon, nor burned by fire, nor moistened by water, nor withered by the wind.
Example: Car and a driver.

  1. A car can not run without a driver.(Body can not live without a soul)
  2. An immature child does not know every car needs a driver.(You may lead your entire life and die without realizing that there is an Atman inside you, which is striving for liberation)

Reincarnation and Sansar/Samsara

Upon death of physical body, the soul discards and enters the new body, depending upon

  1. State of mind at death.
  2. Persons unfulfilled desires.
  3. His karmic record.


Sansar is the process of passing from one body to another,throughout all species of life. Consciousness is present in all form of life but its active use can be only done in human form.
Six broad ascending classes are:

  1. Aquatic
  2. Plants
  3. Reptiles and insects
  4. Birds
  5. Animals
  6. Humans

Law of Karma

Free choice exits only in human form, because all other are guided by instincts. So 

 human life = Life of responsibility

The Karma is action and thought. Each Karma has a reaction (every action has a reaction(Newton)). All positive actions generate good Karma. And Negative generates bad Karma . Everything is recorded in your Karmic balance. 

 Karma     Nikarma     Akarma
   ↓          ↓          ↓
 Elevate   Degrade     Neutral

Movement of Atman is decided by the Karmic balance. Karma is generated only in the human life. Lower life forms are burning the bad Karma and higher life forms(Devas) are using the good Karma before becoming the humans again.

Key issues

Punya(good Karma) and paap(bad Karma).

Extreme examples:

  1. Naga Sadhus : Through severe penance, all Karmas are to washed away, to achieve Moksha.
  2. Even the lord is not immune from the law of Karma:
    1. Lord Shri Krishna was cursed by Gandhari due to his Karmas(good and bad) and that led to his death
    2. The Lord Vishnu was curded by the Vrinda((tulsi)wife of Jalandhar), so the Ram and Sita avatar happened.

Prakriti(matter) and Guna

  1. Atman is trapped inside successive temporary bodies made of Prakriti.
  2. Matter is inert, temporary and unconscious.
  3. Matter is (3 stages):
    1. Created → Rajas(guna)→ passion
    2. Remains → Sattva(gunna)→ goodness
    3. destroyed → Tamas (gunna)→ ignorance
      S सत्त्व(Shiva) → goodness → pure,elevating → (real knowledge)
R रजस्(Vishnu) → passion → Create,acquire,enjoy→ (greed)
S तमस्(Bhrama) → ignorance → dirty,diluting → (madness)

      It is the soul's choice to select Sattva to reach heavenly planets at deaths,Rajas to stay back in human society. and Tamas to enter the lower species.
      Moksha is for the souls going above the level of Sattva.

Four Varnas

  1. Brahmins → Sattva → Priests,teachers,intellectual
  2. Kshatriyas → Sattva + Rajas → Police,solders,administrators(IAS,IPS,ect)
  3. Vaishyas → Rajas + Tamas → Farmers,traders,merchants
  4. Shudras → Tamas → Workers,labors,artisans
