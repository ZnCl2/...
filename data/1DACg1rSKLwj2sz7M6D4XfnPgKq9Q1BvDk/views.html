My other site elaborates more or less on my political beliefs and my attitude towards various things. The I2P mirror for Red Liberty (formerly known as Thought Foundry Blog) can be found here.

Generally it boils down to a few things:

-The internet should be decentralized and privacy respecting by default. Transparency for the powerful, privacy for everyone else.

-The internet and society as a whole should not rely on undemocratic corporations or state power (both are usually bad).

-Democracy should be face-to-face, real politics as opposed to statecraft (DuckDuckGo Murray Bookchin)

-Democratize the enterprise! Bring all major corporations under democratic control, industrial democracy (socialism) as a necessary prerequisite to grassroots political democracy

-Unlimited individual liberty, freedom is always the freedom of the dissenters. Democracy and open society should be saved.

-Positive Liberty is necessary, we must grow the tree of Liberty (it was as absurd to speak of a right to healthcare in 1776 as it was to speak of freedom of the press in pre-literate society).

-In a world that produces enough food to feed 10 billion people a year, the existence of world hunger is a crime against humanity.

-When there are 5 empty homes for every homeless person in the wealthiest country in the world, the existence of homelessness is a crime against humanity.

-The Book of Acts is the first real "Communist Manifesto". God is love.

-There is no such thing as green capitalism. Socialism or barbarism.

-All drugs should be legal and regulated. Consenting adults want what they want and this is a personal matter. But at the same time healthy adults should look into taking psychedelics at least once in their lives. Turn on, Tune in, Drop out.

-Against dogmatism and political religions. Don't take yourself too seriously, always admit everything you "know" could be wrong. 20th century was a nightmare, we can do better. The methods must embody the aims.

-Political correctness is often counter-productive (see Slavoj Zizek).

-Don't be mean to people or disrespect them just because they have different political/religious views than you. Follow the golden rule.

-Other libertarian socialist stuff. Check out Red Liberty!
